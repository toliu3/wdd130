Foreword

Mr. Liu Wanlai is an enthusiastic collector of genealogy. In a gathering in February 1967, Liu Wanlai proposed to write the Liu family tree and build an ancestral hall to commemorate the ancestors. After everyone agreed, Liu Wanlai, Liu Yishou, and Liu Mingjiao were recommended to be responsible for the construction work and organize the preparatory committee. The ground was broken in August 1967 through the enthusiastic donations of many clan relatives, and the construction was completed in March 1968. The address is located in Huayuanli, Guanmiao District, Tainan City. The purpose of this ancestral hall is to commemorate the ancestors who came to Taiwan from mainland China in the early 18th century and to remember the spirit of hardship and adventure of the ancestors. At the same time, it also commemorates the ancestors who lived here. Use this ancestral hall to let the clan members understand the deeds of their ancestors, and at the same time, it can also connect the feelings of clan relatives from all over the world.

History

The ancestor Liu Wanxing was born on April 24, 1707. He originally lived in Tangyuan Society, Tong'an County, Quanzhou Prefecture, Fujian Province, China, and lived in 19 Duxiangzikou, Horseshoe Society (about now Longhai District, Zhangzhou City, Fujian Province, China). In the early 18th century, together with Liu Yuan and Liu Wande, they came to Tainan, Taiwan from China to reclaim wasteland and make a living by farming. From then to now, their descendants in Taiwan have been 12 generations. The number of descendants is close to a thousand. Most of the descendants still live in the same place.

Activity

The ancestral hall established a management committee in 1967. The first chairman was Liu Wanlai, the second chairman was Liu Qizuo, the third chairman was Liu Hetian, and now the fourth chairman is Liu Jianming. Liu Wanlai collected the family tree and compiled a family tree of the Liu surname, which was published in 1993. All genealogical information has been stored on the FamilySearch website, and interested people can visit the website for inquiries. Since Mr. Liu Wanxing, the first ancestor who came to Taiwan, was born on March 22 of the lunar calendar, in the past, the ancestral hall of the Liu surname used March 22 of the lunar calendar every year as a day for the Liu surname clan to gather and commemorate the ancestors. Due to social changes, in order to facilitate clan gatherings, the current date of commemoration has been changed to the Saturday before March 22 in the lunar calendar. The commemoration ceremony will start at 10:00 in the morning, and the clan will gather together in the evening for a meal. In a year, this has become the most important activity of the Liu ancestral hall. During the dinner, the management committee will also award scholarships to students with excellent grades in the clan to encourage children to study hard at school.
